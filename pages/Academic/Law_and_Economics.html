៥. ជំនាញវិជ្ជាជីវៈ

កម្មវិធីនេះរៀបចំឡើងក្នុងគោលបំណងសម្រួលអ្នកសិក្សាឲ្យទទួលបានជំនាញពិតប្រាកដ ដោយផ្សារភ្ជាប់ទ្រឹស្តីនិងអនុវត្ត...

  • Financial Management and Analysis
  • Financial Management and Planning
  • Marketing

Professional Skills

1. Financial Management and Analysis

This course is designed to prepare students to interpret and analyze financial statements for tasks involving credit and security analyses, lending and investment decisions, and other decisions that must rely on financial data. It explores in greater depth financial reporting topics from the perspective of financial statement users. Students develop a sufficient understanding of the concepts and recording procedures to interpret various disclosures in an informed manner. Topics include profitability and risk assessments, competitor analysis, bankruptcy prediction, and valuation.

2. Financial Management and Planning

This course focuses on financial management, showing managers how to interface with accounting and finance departments and helping them understand how firms meet financial objectives using decision-making strategies. It covers financial tools and techniques for maximizing firm value through capital budgeting, capital structure, and working capital management. Topics also include multinational financial management, risk management, and more.

3. Marketing

This course focuses on communicating the value of a product or service to customers to facilitate sales. It covers target market selection, market segmentation, consumer behavior, and value-based advertising. From a societal perspective, marketing links material needs with economic responses and builds long-term relationships through exchange processes.

4. Business Communication

This course includes topics such as marketing, brand management, customer relations, consumer behavior, advertising, public relations, corporate communication, community engagement, and event management. It is closely tied to professional and technical communication, aiming to enhance organizational and interpersonal effectiveness.

5. Business Law

This course covers the rights, relations, and conduct of individuals and businesses involved in commerce, merchandising, trade, and sales. Considered a branch of civil law, it addresses both private and public law issues related to business activities.
